#### 2.2. Generation of a group

*Français : 2.2. Génération d'un groupe*

Dans toute cette partie, \( G \) désigne un groupe, dont l’élément neutre est noté e.

> Throughout this section, \( G \) denotes a group, whose identity element is denoted by e.

DéFINITION 8. Soit \( A \subset G \) . Il existe un plus petit sous-groupe \( H \) de \( G \) contenant \( A \) , qui est l’ensemble des éléments de \( G \) qui s’écrivent comme le produit d’éléments de \( A \) et d’inverses d’éléments de \( A \) . On l’appelle sous-groupe engendré par \( A \) et on note \( H = \langle A\rangle \) . Lorsque l’ensemble \( A = \left\{ {{x}_{1},\ldots ,{x}_{n}}\right\} \) est fini, on note aussi \( H = \left\langle {{x}_{1},\ldots ,{x}_{n}}\right\rangle \) .

> DEFINITION 8. Let \( A \subset G \) . There exists a smallest subgroup \( H \) of \( G \) containing \( A \) , which is the set of elements of \( G \) that can be written as the product of elements of \( A \) and inverses of elements of \( A \) . It is called the subgroup generated by \( A \) and is denoted by \( H = \langle A\rangle \) . When the set \( A = \left\{ {{x}_{1},\ldots ,{x}_{n}}\right\} \) is finite, we also denote it by \( H = \left\langle {{x}_{1},\ldots ,{x}_{n}}\right\rangle \) .

Exemple 4. Pour tout \( a \in G,\langle a\rangle = \left\{ {{a}^{m} \mid m \in \mathbb{Z}}\right\} \) . Si deux éléments \( a \) et \( b \) de \( G \) commutent, alors \( \langle a, b\rangle = \left\{ {{a}^{m}{b}^{n} \mid \left( {m, n}\right) \in {\mathbb{Z}}^{2}}\right\} \) .

> Example 4. For any \( a \in G,\langle a\rangle = \left\{ {{a}^{m} \mid m \in \mathbb{Z}}\right\} \) . If two elements \( a \) and \( b \) of \( G \) commute, then \( \langle a, b\rangle = \left\{ {{a}^{m}{b}^{n} \mid \left( {m, n}\right) \in {\mathbb{Z}}^{2}}\right\} \) .

DéFINITION 9. - Un groupe \( G \) est dit monogène s’il existe \( a \in G \) tel que \( G = \langle a\rangle \) . Si de plus \( G \) est fini, on dit que \( G \) est cyclique.

> DEFINITION 9. - A group \( G \) is said to be monogenic if there exists \( a \in G \) such that \( G = \langle a\rangle \) . If, in addition, \( G \) is finite, we say that \( G \) is cyclic.

- Un groupe \( G \) est dit de type fini s’il existe un nombre fini d’éléments \( {a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n} \) de \( G \) tels que \( G = \left\langle  {{a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n}}\right\rangle \) . Un tel \( n \) -uplet \( \left( {{a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n}}\right) \) est appelé système de générateurs de \( G \) .

> - A group \( G \) is said to be finitely generated if there exists a finite number of elements \( {a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n} \) of \( G \) such that \( G = \left\langle  {{a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n}}\right\rangle \) . Such a \( n \) -tuple \( \left( {{a}_{1},\ldots ,{a}_{n}}\right) \) is called a generating set of \( G \) .

Remarque 6. - Tout groupe monogène est abélien.

> Remark 6. - Every monogenic group is abelian.

- Pour tout entier naturel non nul \( n,\left( {\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, + }\right) \) est un groupe cyclique. De plus, tout groupe cyclique à \( n \) éléments est isomorphe à \( \left( {\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, + }\right) \) .

> - For any non-zero natural number \( n,\left( {\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, + }\right) \) is a cyclic group. Furthermore, every cyclic group with \( n \) elements is isomorphic to \( \left( {\mathbb{Z}/n\mathbb{Z}, + }\right) \) .

DéFINITION 10. Un élément \( a \) de \( G \) est dit d’ordre \( p \in {\mathbb{N}}^{ * } \) si \( \langle a\rangle \) est fini d’ordre \( p \) . L’ordre de \( a \) est aussi le plus petit entier naturel non nul \( p \) tel que \( {a}^{p} = e \) , et on a \( \langle a\rangle = \left\{ {e, a,\ldots ,{a}^{p - 1}}\right\} . \)

> DEFINITION 10. An element \( a \) of \( G \) is said to be of order \( p \in {\mathbb{N}}^{ * } \) if \( \langle a\rangle \) is finite of order \( p \) . The order of \( a \) is also the smallest non-zero natural number \( p \) such that \( {a}^{p} = e \) , and we have \( \langle a\rangle = \left\{ {e, a,\ldots ,{a}^{p - 1}}\right\} . \)

\( \rightarrow \) THÉORÉME 2. Si G est fini d’ordre \( n \) , alors l’ordre de tout élément de \( G \) divise \( n \) . En particulier, tout élément a de \( G \) vérifie \( {a}^{n} = e \) .

> \( \rightarrow \) THEOREM 2. If G is finite of order \( n \) , then the order of any element of \( G \) divides \( n \) . In particular, every element a of \( G \) satisfies \( {a}^{n} = e \) .

THÉORÉME 3. Soit a un élément de \( G \) d’ordre p. On a l’équivalence \( \left( {{a}^{q} = e}\right) \Leftrightarrow \; \left( {p \mid q}\right) \) .

> THEOREM 3. Let a be an element of \( G \) of order p. We have the equivalence \( \left( {{a}^{q} = e}\right) \Leftrightarrow \; \left( {p \mid q}\right) \) .

THÉORÉME 4. Si l'ordre de \( G \) est un nombre premier, le groupe \( G \) est cyclique, engendré par tout élément différent de l'élément neutre.

> THEOREM 4. If the order of \( G \) is a prime number, the group \( G \) is cyclic, generated by any element other than the identity element.

Proposition 5. Si \( G \) est cyclique d’ordre \( n, G = \langle a\rangle \) , alors

> Proposition 5. If \( G \) is cyclic of order \( n, G = \langle a\rangle \) , then

\[
\left( {\left\langle  {a}^{k}\right\rangle   = G}\right) \; \Leftrightarrow  \;\left( {k \land  n = 1}\right) .
\]

Démonstration. Comme \( G = \langle a\rangle \) , l’assertion \( \left\langle {a}^{k}\right\rangle = G \) est équivalente à l’existence d’un entier \( v \) tel que \( {a}^{kv} = a \) . Ceci s’écrit aussi \( {a}^{{kv} - 1} = e \) , ou encore \( n \mid \left( {{kv} - 1}\right) \) , c’est-à-dire \( \exists u, v \in \; \mathbb{Z} \mid {kv} - 1 = {un} \) , ce qui équivaut d’après le théorème de Bezout à \( k \land n = 1 \) .

> Proof. Since \( G = \langle a\rangle \), the assertion \( \left\langle {a}^{k}\right\rangle = G \) is equivalent to the existence of an integer \( v \) such that \( {a}^{kv} = a \). This can also be written as \( {a}^{{kv} - 1} = e \), or \( n \mid \left( {{kv} - 1}\right) \), that is to say \( \exists u, v \in \; \mathbb{Z} \mid {kv} - 1 = {un} \), which is equivalent by Bezout's theorem to \( k \land n = 1 \).
