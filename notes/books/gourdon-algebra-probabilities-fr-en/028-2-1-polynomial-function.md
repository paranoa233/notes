#### 2.1. Polynomial function

*Français : 2.1. Fonction polynôme*

Soit \( A \) une \( \mathbb{K} \) -algèbre (c’est-à-dire un \( \mathbb{K} \) -espace vectoriel muni d’un produit interne - noté ici multiplicativement - faisant de \( A \) un anneau et tel que si \( \lambda \in \mathbb{K} \) , et \( x, y \in A \) , alors \( \lambda \left( {xy}\right) = \left( {\lambda x}\right) y = x\left( {\lambda y}\right) ) \) non nécessairement commutative (par exemple \( A = \mathbb{K} \) , \( A = \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack X\right\rbrack \) ou \( A = {\mathcal{M}}_{n}\left( \mathbb{K}\right) ). \)

> Let \( A \) be a \( \mathbb{K} \) -algebra (i.e., a \( \mathbb{K} \) -vector space equipped with an internal product - denoted here multiplicatively - making \( A \) a ring and such that if \( \lambda \in \mathbb{K} \) , and \( x, y \in A \) , then \( \lambda \left( {xy}\right) = \left( {\lambda x}\right) y = x\left( {\lambda y}\right) ) \) not necessarily commutative (for example \( A = \mathbb{K} \) , \( A = \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack X\right\rbrack \) or \( A = {\mathcal{M}}_{n}\left( \mathbb{K}\right) ). \)

Pour tout \( F = {a}_{0} + {a}_{1}X + \cdots + {a}_{n}{X}^{n} \in \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack X\right\rbrack \) , on note \( \widetilde{F} \) l’application

> For any \( F = {a}_{0} + {a}_{1}X + \cdots + {a}_{n}{X}^{n} \in \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack X\right\rbrack \) , we denote by \( \widetilde{F} \) the map

\[
\widetilde{F} : A \rightarrow  A\;x \mapsto  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{{i = 0}}^{n}{a}_{i}{x}^{i}
\]

- Si \( F, G \in  \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack  X\right\rbrack \) , on a \( \widetilde{F + G} = \widetilde{F} + \widetilde{G} \) et \( \widetilde{FG} = \widetilde{F}\widetilde{G} \) .

> - If \( F, G \in  \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack  X\right\rbrack \) , we have \( \widetilde{F + G} = \widetilde{F} + \widetilde{G} \) and \( \widetilde{FG} = \widetilde{F}\widetilde{G} \) .

- Si \( A = \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack  X\right\rbrack \) , on note \( F \circ  G = \widetilde{F}\left( G\right) \) . Pour toute \( \mathbb{K} \) -algèbre \( A \) , on a alors \( \widetilde{F \circ  G} = \; \widetilde{F} \circ  \widetilde{G} \) .

> - If \( A = \mathbb{K}\left\lbrack  X\right\rbrack \) , we denote \( F \circ  G = \widetilde{F}\left( G\right) \) . For any \( \mathbb{K} \) -algebra \( A \) , we then have \( \widetilde{F \circ  G} = \; \widetilde{F} \circ  \widetilde{G} \) .

Remarque 1. En général il n’y a pas d’ambiguïté, la fonction polynôme \( x \mapsto \widetilde{F}\left( x\right) \) est notée plus simplement \( x \mapsto F\left( x\right) \) .

> Remark 1. In general there is no ambiguity, the polynomial function \( x \mapsto \widetilde{F}\left( x\right) \) is denoted more simply as \( x \mapsto F\left( x\right) \) .
