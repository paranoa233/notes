#### 4.1. Generalities

*Français : 4.1. Généralités*

DéFINITION 1. On appelle forme linéaire sur \( E \) toute application linéaire de \( E \) dans \( \mathbb{K} \) . L’ensemble \( \mathcal{L}\left( {E,\mathbb{K}}\right) \) des formes linéaires sur \( E \) est aussi noté \( {E}^{ * } \) . C’est un \( \mathbb{K} \) -e.v appelé espace dual de \( E \) .

> DEFINITION 1. A linear form on \( E \) is any linear map from \( E \) to \( \mathbb{K} \) . The set \( \mathcal{L}\left( {E,\mathbb{K}}\right) \) of linear forms on \( E \) is also denoted by \( {E}^{ * } \) . It is a \( \mathbb{K} \) -vector space called the dual space of \( E \) .

Notation. Si \( x \in E \) et \( \varphi \in {E}^{ * } \) , on note parfois \( \varphi \left( x\right) = \langle \varphi , x\rangle \) .

> Notation. If \( x \in E \) and \( \varphi \in {E}^{ * } \), we sometimes denote it \( \varphi \left( x\right) = \langle \varphi , x\rangle \).

DéFINITION 2. On appelle bidual de \( E \) l’espace dual de \( {E}^{ * } \) , noté \( {E}^{* * } \) .

> DEFINITION 2. The bidual of \( E \) is the dual space of \( {E}^{ * } \), denoted \( {E}^{* * } \).
