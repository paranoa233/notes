#### 2.6. Product of a polynomial and an exponential

*Français : 2.6. Produit d'un polynôme et d'une exponentielle*

Les primitives \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}{dx} \) où \( P \) est un polynôme et \( r \) un nombre complexe non nul s’écrivent sous la forme \( Q\left( x\right) {e}^{rx} + k \) où \( Q \) est un polynôme ayant même degré que \( P \) et \( k \) une constante. On trouve les coefficients de \( Q \) par identification dans l’expression \( {rQ} + {Q}^{\prime } = P. \)

> Antiderivatives of \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}{dx} \) where \( P \) is a polynomial and \( r \) is a non-zero complex number are written in the form \( Q\left( x\right) {e}^{rx} + k \) where \( Q \) is a polynomial of the same degree as \( P \) and \( k \) is a constant. The coefficients of \( Q \) are found by identification in the expression \( {rQ} + {Q}^{\prime } = P. \)

Les primitives \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}\cos {mxdx} \) ou \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}\sin {mxdx}\left( {r, m \in \mathbb{R}, P\text{ polynôme }}\right) \) s’obtiennent en les écrivant comme les parties réelles et imaginaires de \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{\left( {r + {im}}\right) x}{dx} \) .

> Antiderivatives of \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}\cos {mxdx} \) or \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{rx}\sin {mxdx}\left( {r, m \in \mathbb{R}, P\text{ polynôme }}\right) \) are obtained by writing them as the real and imaginary parts of \( \int P\left( x\right) {e}^{\left( {r + {im}}\right) x}{dx} \).
