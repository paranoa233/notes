# A.9 Cohomology with Compact Support

A cochain \( c \in  {C}^{i}M \) is said to have compact support if there exists a compact set \( K \subset  M \) so that \( c \) belongs to the submodule \( {C}^{i}\left( {M, M - K}\right)  \subset  {C}^{i}M \) . In other words \( c \) must annihilate every singular simplex in \( M - K \) . The cochains with compact support form a submodule which will be denoted by \( {C}_{c}^{i}M \subset  {C}^{i}M \) . The cohomology groups of this complex \( {C}_{c}^{ \bullet  }M \) will be denoted by \( {\mathrm{H}}_{c}^{i}M \) . A straightforward argument [Spa81, p. 162] shows that \( {\mathrm{H}}_{c}^{i}M \) is isomorphic to the direct limit of the groups \( {\mathrm{H}}^{i}\left( {M, M - K}\right) \) as \( K \) varies over the directed set consisting of all compact subsets of \( M \) . If \( M \) is compact, note that \( {\mathrm{H}}_{c}^{i}M \cong  {\mathrm{H}}^{i}M \) .

If \( M \) is oriented, then there is an important homomorphism

\[
{\mathrm{H}}_{c}^{n}M \rightarrow  \Lambda
\]

which will be denoted by \( a \mapsto  a\left\lbrack  M\right\rbrack \) , and called integration over \( M \) . When \( M \) is compact, this can be defined by

\[
a\left\lbrack  M\right\rbrack   = \left\langle  {a,{\mu }_{M}}\right\rangle   \in  \Lambda .
\]

In the general case it is necessary to choose some representative \( {a}^{\prime } \in  {\mathrm{H}}^{n}\left( {M, M - K}\right) \) for \( a \) , and then to define

\[
a\left\lbrack  M\right\rbrack   = \left\langle  {{a}^{\prime },{\mu }_{K}}\right\rangle  .
\]

The reader should verify that this definition does not depend on the choice of \( K \) and \( {a}^{\prime } \) .
